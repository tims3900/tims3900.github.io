3D U-Net for Airway Segmentation

UROP Fellowship Project - Molloi Lab, UCI

Built a 3D U-Net from scratch to automatically segment airways from CT scans, focusing on sub-2mm structures critical for detecting early-stage lung diseases like COPD and asthma. Achieved a Dice coefficient of 0.94 on limited training data.

Technical Details:

3D Architecture: Implemented full 3D convolutional pipeline with encoder-decoder structure and skip connections. Used 3×3×3 kernels throughout to capture spatial relationships that 2D methods miss in complex branching airway structures.

Data Challenge Solution: With only 25 training volumes (285MB each), developed smart preprocessing including lung cropping, 256×256×256 patch generation, and automated trachea removal to address data imbalance.

Trachea Removal Algorithm: Created slice-by-slice analysis to identify and remove overrepresented tracheal structures, allowing the model to focus on clinically important smaller airways instead of being biased toward large structures.

Results

Achieved Dice coefficient of 0.94, competitive with published methods
Successfully segmented complex 3D branching airway structures
Contributed to research accepted for 2025 AAPM Annual Meeting
