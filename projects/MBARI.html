Kelp Coverage Calculation from UAV Imagery

MBARI Summer Internship Project

Developed an automated pipeline to calculate kelp canopy coverage from standard RGB drone imagery, eliminating the need for expensive multi-spectral cameras. Processed 26,000+ high-resolution images across 6 Monterey Bay sites to monitor kelp forest health.

Technical Details:

Problem Inversion: Instead of segmenting fragmented kelp patches directly, the uniform water background was segmented instead and mask was inverted - much more reliable since water appears as cohesive regions.

SAM + SAHI Integration: Combined Segment Anything Model with Slicing Aided Hyper Inference to process 42-megapixel images efficiently. The pipeline automatically slices large images into manageable patches with intelligent overlap handling.

Smart Seed Point Generation: Developed automated algorithms to place optimal seed points for SAM using color analysis and Poisson disk sampling, ensuring comprehensive water region capture.

Results:

Successfully processed 26,000+ images from 6 survey sites
Created ground truth dataset through manual annotation and vegetation indices
